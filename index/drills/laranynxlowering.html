Yawning

The yawning drill uses the mechanism of yawning to help familiarize oneself with the sensation of lowering the larynx and how the various muscles that manipulate it move it downward.

How to Preform This Drill

First yawn and hold your muscles in place. Feel where your laranyx (adams apple) is located with your hand. Feel wheat muscles are relaxed and what musles are tense, causing it to be in this lowered state. Now, yawn onces more, keeping your laranyx in place as you vocalize or sing a line or two. The piece that you practices should while lowering your larynx in this way should not be long.

A Note About This Drill

This drill can be straining and damaging to your voice if done to often and repeatedly. It's only intended to teach you how to recognize what is being moved and causing the laranyx to lower. For safer, more long term practice instead look toward the small dog/big dog drill.

Small Dog/Big Dog

The "Small Dog/Big Dog drill" is similar to the "Big Dog/Small Dog drill used to practice raising the layrnx with the only key difference being the order of the pants and direction you work towards. This drill consists of three parts. The Pant, the vocalization and the phonation.

Panting

Start with making a panting sound at your base speaking volume. Feel how open your quotient is and how there is almost no vibration from your vocal cords. For this part, pant three times with the first two pants being the same pitch and the last pant being lower. Continue this until you reach the limit of what you cacn do in two to three cycles and then proceed to the vocalization test.

Vocalization

The vocaliztaion portion is the simplest part of the exercise and simply requires holding as low of a note as you can without raising your larynx or vocal fry. Hold it for two to three seconds and then proceed with the Phonation Test.

Vocalization Test

The vocalization portion of the "Small Dog/Big Dog" exercise is exactly the same as "Big Dog/ Small Dog" save for the difference of working with a a lowered laranyx and attempting to maintain it instead of a raised laranyx. Select a short passage or a few lines from a song and attempt to read or sing it aloud while maintaining the lowered laranyx.
