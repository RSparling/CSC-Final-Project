The 'ee-oo' Drill

The 'ee-oo' is a simple drill that exploits the natural manner in which most people attempt to pronounce the vowel sounds 'ee' and 'oo'. The 'ee' sound is produced by raising the back of the tongue and the 'oo' sound is produced by lowering the back of the tongue.

First, make the 'ee' sound like in the word 'see'. Notice how the back of the tongue raises. Next, make the 'oo' sound like in the word 'boo'. Notice how the back of the tongue lowerrs and the mouth opens up and enlarges. Having these two shape in mind, try to make the 'ee' sound while keeping your mouth liek for the 'oo' sound. Make sure to try to shift away from the 'oo' shape as little as possible.

Another things that you can do is maintain the 'ee' or 'oo' sound while moving the shape of your lips, changing lip tightness, moving your jaw and moving your tongue. The purpose of this is to explore the parameters you have to affect the sounds your make.

The Plosive/Fricitive Drill

The Plosive/Fricitive Drill is a simple drill that allows you to explore the effect of changing the embrachure on the sound produced. This is done via using constant soudns and chagnging the shape of the mouth.

First make a 'p' sound like in the word 'pop'. Notice how the lips come together, how tight they are when you make the sound, the force of the air inside the mouth. Next, make a 'f' sound like in the word 'fog'. Focus on the effect the lips, how tightly you press you teeth against your lips, and how your tongue is positioned.

Then play with these two sounds, change any aspect or parameter of your mouth you can think of. Lip tighness, how raised or lowered the tongue is, how far forward or back the tongue is and how it moves as your produce a sound.
