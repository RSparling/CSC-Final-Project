Embouchure

In the context of speaking, the embouchure refers to the way in which the lips, mouth and facial muscles are used to shape and produce sound. While the term is often used in relation to wind insturments it can also be used in speaking and voice acting, impacting how the voice sounds as we comminicate. A more controlled embouchure can help to enunciate more with clear sounds and words however it can also have an affect on the voice useful for the purpose of voice acting.

Large Embochure

A lareg embochure involves a broader mouth opening and more relaxes lip positioning. This resuts in a fuller sound and enhances vocal projection. however the openess can also all more air to escape making a more breathy voice. It is imporatant to note that breathy is not always a bad thing but is not the same thing as an open quotient. A large embochure can also give a sensation of "size" to the voice, making it sound larger or from a larger character.

You can hear the effect of a large embochure in some actors or characters to include:

  • James Earl Jones
  • Patrick Stewart
  • Idris Elba

Small Embocure

Through the narrowing of the embochure with a smaller mouth opening and more tense lip position, a voice can sound more crisp, focused or brighter. This can come accross more "consentrated", or "sharp". This is another effect often used in the voiceacting of children, women and characters that are trying to be portrayed as "small" or "cute". Often this is can be heard in various cartoons and anime where voice actors are attempting to portray younger characters.

Examples in Anime

  • Yuki Kaji
  • Maaya Sakamoto
  • Yui Ishikawa

Examples in Actors and People

  • Michael J. Fox
  • Scarlett Johansson
  • Tom Holland
