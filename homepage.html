Our Goal

The goal of this website is to enable people to take control of their voice and equip them with the tools needed to train and refine it how they see fit.

How to Best Use this Site

This site is best used as a refrence for both learning about a vocal element and how to learn some basic ways to control that element. And while this has some openly available knowledge currated and distilled down to make it more digestable and easy to understand know that it's not an end-all-be-all. You should still look elsewhere to expand your repretoire of tools and techniques.

Tips for Effective Practice

When practicing you should make sure to keep a glass of water nearby. While many say warm water is prefered any water is fine so long as it's not ice water as that can cause your vocal cords to lose a bit of elasticity and make you more prone to injurty.

Additionally, make sure to stop practicing if you experience any pain or moderate to severe discomfort. While your voice is a tool and instrument it is also a muscle (or a collection of them) and can be injured and even irrevercably damaged if pushed too far.

Finally, make sure to practice often. Studies show that smaller but more regular practice sessions help individuals to learn better than rarer but much longer sessions. Jusy taking 15 minutes out of your day makes a massive difference in the long run.
